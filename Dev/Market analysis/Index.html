India Has 3% of Global DC Capacity Despite 20% of Global Data
▾

India generates approximately 20% of the world's data — driven by its 1.4 billion population and world-leading mobile data consumption — yet holds just 3% of global data centre capacity. This is the structural mismatch Antriksh resolves.

  • India's mobile data consumption: 26 EB/month — equal to China, 2.6× the USA
  • India DC capacity: 854 MW (2023), up from 350 MW (2019)
  • Needs 20,000–25,000 MW of AI-ready capacity by 2030
  • Current trajectory delivers <3,000 MW — a 7–8× shortfall
⚡
GPU Rack Density Doubling Every 2–3 Years
▾

Traditional data centres are physically incapable of hosting modern AI GPUs. Rack density requirements have exploded, making legacy infrastructure obsolete for AI workloads.

  • Average DC power density doubled: 8 kW → 17 kW per rack
  • AI-ready DCs expected to reach 30 kW per rack by 2027
  • NVIDIA GB200 NVL72 requires 120+ kW per rack
  • Antriksh supports 150–300 kW per rack via liquid cooling
  • Air-cooled legacy DCs throttle GPU performance by 15–30%
🏛️
Government Policy Strongly Supports Domestic AI Infrastructure
▾

The Indian government has recognised AI data centres as critical infrastructure and created a favourable policy environment for domestic investment.

  • ₹10,732 crore ($1.24B) allocated for India AI infrastructure
  • Infrastructure status granted to the data centre sector (Draft DC Policy 2020)
  • DPDP Act mandates data localisation for sensitive categories
  • US GPU export restrictions lifted — direct allocation now possible
  • SEZ tax exemptions and capital subsidies available for qualifying projects
